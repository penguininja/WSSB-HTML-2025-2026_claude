Encyclopedia of Nature: Giant Pandas

Introduction

Quick Facts:
  • Status: Vulnerable
  • Diet: Bamboo
  • Region: China

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear, is a bear species endemic to China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body. Though it belongs to the order Carnivora, the giant panda is a folivore, with bamboo shoots and leaves making up more than 99% of its diet. Giant pandas in the wild will occasionally eat other grasses, wild tubers, or even meat in the form of birds, rodents, or carrion.

History

For many decades, the precise taxonomic classification of the giant panda was under debate because it shares characteristics with both bears and raccoons. However, molecular studies indicate the giant panda is a true bear, part of the family Ursidae.

Conservation

Giant pandas are considered an endangered species. This means without human help they are at risk of extinction. There are efforts by the World Wildlife Foundation (WWF) and other organizations to help conserve giant panda habitats, to ensure these beautiful creatures continue to survive.
